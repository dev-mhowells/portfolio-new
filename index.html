Messenger for language learners

Allows teachers to correct messages from students. Students can also easily call attention to words they don’t know. Teachers can then provide synonyms, examples and pronunciation for these words through text and audio.

Made with: HTML, CSS, Tailwind, JavaScript, React and Firebase.

English Deck

An article with built in flashcards, a quiz, and comment section. Flashcards can be flipped, cycled through and saved for review. A quiz at the end checks article comprehension. Readers can log in and practice using key words in the comment section, which checks off these words as they are typed by the user.

Made with: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React and Firebase.

Beginnings

I started coding because I wanted to program my own psychology experiments for academic research. By the time I had met my goal of learning enough Python to feasibly do so, I realised I was far more interested in the coding than the research! I moved into web development because I wanted the things I created to reach a wider audience.

Psychology and the web

While completing an MSc in psychology, I conducted research on the relationship between behavioural addiction, cognition, and online gambling related technologies. I have always been interested in the relationship between psychology and technology, and I’m currently learning more about the role of psychology in UX design.

Online learning

As an ex English language teacher and a fan of self-directed learning, I love e-learning platforms. I speak conversational Korean, in part thanks to some amazing online resources. I had previously worked on a wordpress website with interactive articles to help English learners with reading comprehension. However, I was frustrated with the limitations of the available plug-ins designed for e-learning sites, so I decided to start working on my own!
